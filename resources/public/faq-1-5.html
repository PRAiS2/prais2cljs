UNDERSTANDING CHILDREN’S HEART SURGERY OUTCOMES

    Where did the formula used to calculate the predicted range come from?

    The statistical formula comes from a method called “Partial Risk Adjustment in Surgery” or PRAiS for short, developed by researchers at Great Ormond Street Hospital and University College London.

    The national audit body uses a formula developed by researchers at Great Ormond Street Hospital and University College London called PRAiS (Partial Risk Adjustment in Surgery - see also the What, why, how? section). The underlying methodology of this method is published in the academic literature if you are interested in learning more details.
